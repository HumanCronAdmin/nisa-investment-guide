US Tax Obligations? Read This First

If you are a US citizen, green card holder, or otherwise have US tax filing obligations, Japanese investment accounts have significant implications:

  • NISA is NOT tax-free for you. The US does not recognize NISA's tax-exempt status. All gains, dividends, and interest must be reported on your US return.
  • iDeCo contributions are NOT deductible on your US return. Withdrawals may be taxed by both countries (treaty relief may apply).
  • PFIC rules apply. Japanese mutual funds (toushin) are classified as Passive Foreign Investment Companies. This can result in punitive US taxation. US-listed ETFs (e.g., VT, VTI) held through an overseas broker avoid this issue.
  • FBAR and FATCA reporting required. If your combined foreign accounts exceed $10,000 at any point, file FinCEN 114 (FBAR). FATCA Form 8938 may also be required.
  • Foreign tax credits can offset double taxation, but the calculation is complex. Use Form 1116.

This is not tax advice. Consult a US-Japan cross-border tax specialist. Common recommendations include: use iDeCo with caution, avoid Japanese mutual funds (use US-listed ETFs instead), and always file both returns.

Investment Options in Detail

iDeCo

Individual Defined Contribution Pension

Japan's tax-advantaged retirement savings plan. Contributions reduce your taxable income, and investment gains grow tax-free until withdrawal at age 60+. Monthly contribution limits apply (e.g., ¥23,000 for most company employees).

Pros

  • Tax-deductible contributions
  • Tax-free growth during accumulation
  • Retirement income deduction at withdrawal
  • Wide range of low-cost index funds

Cons

  • Cannot withdraw until age 60
  • Low monthly contribution limits
  • Management fees vary by provider

Taxable Brokerage Account

Tokutei Koza (Special Account)

A standard taxable account at a Japanese broker. No contribution limits, full liquidity, and the option for automatic tax withholding. Gains are taxed at a flat 20.315%.

Pros

  • No contribution limits
  • Withdraw anytime
  • Automatic tax withholding option
  • Wide range of investment products

Cons

  • Gains taxed at 20.315%
  • No tax deduction on contributions
  • Dividends also taxed

Overseas Brokerage

e.g., Interactive Brokers

Access US ETFs and international markets directly. Often lower expense ratios, but requires self-filing taxes in Japan and carries currency exchange risk.

Pros

  • Direct access to US ETFs with ultra-low fees
  • Broader investment universe
  • Multi-currency accounts
  • Fractional shares available

Cons

  • Currency exchange risk (JPY/USD)
  • Must file annual tax return
  • US dividend withholding tax (10%)
  • Foreign asset reporting requirements

Savings & Government Bonds

Bank Deposits & JGBs

The safest option with principal protection. Bank deposits are insured up to ¥10 million per institution. Returns are minimal but capital is secure.

Pros

  • Principal protection
  • Deposit insurance up to ¥10M
  • High liquidity
  • Government-backed bonds

Cons

  • Very low returns (often below inflation)
  • Interest taxed at 20.315%
  • Significant opportunity cost
  • JGBs have 1-year minimum hold
